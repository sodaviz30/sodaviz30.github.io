The Risky Nightlife:
Analysis of Crimes in SF during the Late Hours

As the sun goes down, the bustling and commotion of the day quiets down, and San Francisco transforms into a place with a rich nightlife - but with an equally increased risk of danger. This report highlights the temporal crime patterns of San Francisco's neighbourhoods, and crunches data from more than two decades, to better guide residents and tourists toward staying safe.

Background

Protecting yourself against crime in San Fransisco

The city San Francisco is celebrated for its vibrant culture, iconic skyline, and restless energy, the passage from day to night brings a subtle but meaningful shift. As the sun dips behind the Pacific and neighbourhoods transition from bustling daytime activity to the glow of nightlife patterns of movement, behavior, and interactions begin to change. Streets that once carried commuters and tourists take on new roles—hosting late-night diners, bar crowds, service workers, and those navigating the city after dark. With this shift in activity, there is also a shift in the risk that comes with moving around the city. And although crimerates are falling in the area, it is still necessary to be aware of your surroundings.

This project explores over two decades of crime data in San Francisco, spanning from 2003 to 2025, with a specific focus on how incidents are distributed across time. The data contains information about what kind of incident took place, and when and where it happened. It covers many different types of crimes, but in this project, the focus is on Assault, Larceny/Theft, Robbery, and Motor Vehicle Theft. Rather than assuming that darkness inherently breeds disorder, the analysis seeks to uncover whether these crimes truly intensifies at night, or if the reality is shaped by more complex social, economic, and environmental factors. By examining temporal trends alongside neighbourhood-level variation, this investigation aims to identify recurring patterns. This site aims to inform the visitors:

"Does crime increase during nightlife hours? And where are you most exposed?"

To crime rates in San Francisco, we will analyze the temporal distribution of incidents across the aforementioned crimes, and look at the different districts in San Francisco, to guide the reader on how best to protect themselves.

Trend Analysis

Crime Trends Over Time

"How has crime evolved over time, and which patterns stand out across two decades of data?"

Understanding long-term developments in crime provides insight into broader social and urban dynamics. By examining incident counts over time, we can identify whether crime levels are increasing, decreasing, or shifting across categories. Figure 1 presents these trends from 2003 to 2025, highlighting how different types of crime have evolved.

As illustrated in Figure 1, larceny/theft clearly dominates across the entire period, consistently accounting for the largest share of reported incidents. While relatively stable in the early 2000s, larceny rises sharply after 2012 and peaks in the late 2010s, before dropping noticeably around 2020 and partially recovering thereafter. Assault follows a more gradual upward trend, increasing steadily over time with only minor fluctuations, suggesting a consistent rise rather than sudden shifts. Motor vehicle theft shows greater volatility: after declining in the mid-2000s, it begins to rise again in the 2010s, indicating a possible change in underlying conditions or enforcement patterns. Robbery remains comparatively stable throughout, with smaller increases that do not match the scale of other crime categories. A clear disruption is visible around 2020, where multiple crime types decline simultaneously. This likely reflects broader societal changes during that period, such as reduced mobility and altered daily routines such as corona virus. Overall, the data suggests that while long-term growth is evident in several categories, crime trends are not uniform and are influenced by both structural changes and short-term external factors.

In most districts assault is more likely to happen in the late afternoon, larceny/theft and motor vehicle theft moslty happens around 6 pm, and robbery happens most often at around 8-9 pm. It is therefore good to be even more aware of your surroundings at these times.

Patterns Throughout the Week

Temporal Crime Patterns: Incidents Throughout the Week

As established, crime trends have gone up and down over time, but the weekly patterns can give insights as to which times residents and tourists should be extra careful. Figure 3 illustrates how average incident counts correlate with each hour of the week; both with a crime type perspective, and for each of the ten SF districts.



As can be seen in Figure 3, there are quite clear patterns of sinusoidally increasing and decreasing incidents throughout the days of the week. Notably, looking at Figure 3a, robberies generally happen more, the later in the day it is, and peaks around 10 pm most days, except for weekends, where more incidents happen and peaks around 1-2 am. Throughout the week assaults also tend to peak later in the day, but closer to 4-5 pm, again except for weekends, where they peak around midnight to 1 am. Violent crimes such as assault and robbery have been shown to have increased rates during the evening.[8]

Looking at Figure 3b, we see that different districts have different weekly patterns. For some districts, such as Central and Southern, crimes also tend to be more likely at the late hours of the day. Knowing that different neighbourhoods are characterised by different crime types is important, when deciding how to best stay safe.
